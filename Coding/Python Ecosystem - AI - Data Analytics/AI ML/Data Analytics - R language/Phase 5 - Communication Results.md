# Phase 5 – Communicate Results

- **Definition:** After executing models, the team compares the outcomes against **predefined success and failure criteria**.  
- The team considers how best to **present findings** to various stakeholders, keeping in mind **assumptions and limitations**.  
- Key objectives:
  - Identify **key findings** from the analysis.  
  - **Quantify business value** of the results.  
  - Develop a **narrative** to clearly convey insights to stakeholders.  

---
# Key Activities
- **Result evaluation:** Assess model performance against success criteria.  
- **Visualization:** Use charts, graphs, and dashboards to present results effectively.  
- **Reporting:** Create concise and meaningful reports or presentations for stakeholders.  
- **Storytelling:** Translate analytical outcomes into actionable business insights.  

---
# Traditional Tools
- **Excel / Spreadsheets** – pivot tables, charts, and summary reports  
- **PowerPoint / Keynote** – presentations for stakeholders  
- **STATISTICA / SPSS** – reporting and statistical visualization  

---
# Modern Tools
- **Business Intelligence (BI) tools:** Tableau, Power BI, QlikView  
- **Python/R visualization libraries:** matplotlib, seaborn, plotly, ggplot2  
- **Interactive dashboards:** Shiny (R), Dash (Python), Google Data Studio  
- **Collaboration & documentation:** Notion, Confluence, Jupyter Notebooks  

> Modern tools enable **dynamic visualization, interactive dashboards, and collaborative storytelling**, while traditional tools work well for **static reporting and smaller datasets**.
