# What is Big Data?
- **Big Data** refers to datasets that are **huge in volume** and **grow exponentially with time**.
- It involves analyzing large and complex data sets **computationally** to reveal **patterns, trends, and associations**.

### Examples
- The **New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)** generates about **1 TB of new trade data per day**.
- Social media platforms like **Instagram** and **Facebook** continuously produce massive volumes of data.

---
# Characteristics of Big Data - **5 V's of Big Data**

| **Characteristic** | **Description**                                                            | **Example / Explanation**                                                                                   |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Volume**         | Refers to how much data is generated and stored.                           | Measured in **TB**, **PB**, **EB**, etc.                                                                    |
| **Velocity**       | The speed at which data is generated, collected, and processed.            | Data from **social media posts**, **financial transactions**, or **IoT sensors** are produced in real time. |
| **Variety**        | Represents the different **forms and types of data**.                      | Text, images, videos, audio, logs, etc.                                                                     |
| **Veracity**       | Refers to the **accuracy, reliability, and quality** of the captured data. | Ensuring data is **authentic**, **consistent**, and **free from bias or errors**.                           |
| **Value**          | The **usefulness** or **benefit** derived from analyzing data.             | Turning data insights into **business strategies**, **innovations**, or **decisions**.                      |

# Fields that Generate Data
- **Social Networking Sites** — e.g., Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter)
- **Search Engines** — e.g., Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo
- **Medical History** — Electronic health records, diagnostic data, wearable health devices
- **Online Shopping** — Purchase history, product reviews, clickstream data
- **Stock Exchange** — Real-time trade transactions, market data feeds
