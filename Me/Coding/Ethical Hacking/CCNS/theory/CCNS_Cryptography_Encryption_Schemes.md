# Encryption Schemes

#### Unconditionally Secure
- Cannot be broken even with infinite computing power
- Ciphertext reveals no information about plaintext
- e.g. One-Time Pad (OTP)
- Impractical — key must be as long as message and never reused

#### Computationally Secure
- Cannot be broken with available resources in reasonable time
- Not theoretically unbreakable — just practically infeasible
- e.g. AES-256 — brute force would take longer than age of universe
