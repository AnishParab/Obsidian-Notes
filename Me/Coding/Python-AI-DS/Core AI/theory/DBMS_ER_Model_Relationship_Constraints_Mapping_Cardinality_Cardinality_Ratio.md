# Mapping Cardinality / Cardinality Ratio

- Number of entities to which another entity can be associated via a relationship.

---
# One to One (1:1)

- Entity A associates with at most one entity in B, where A and B are entity sets. And an entity of B is associated with at most one entity of A.
- Example: Citizen has Aadhar Card.

![[one_to_one_er_model.excalidraw|400]]

---
# One to Many (1:N)

- Entity in A associated with N entity in B. While entity in B is associated with at most one entity in A.
- Example: Citizen has Vehicle.

![[one_to_many_er_model.excalidraw|400]]

---
# Many to One (N:1)

- Entity in A associated with at most one entityin B. While entity in B can be associated with N entity in A. 
- Example: Course taken ny Professor.

![[many_to_one_er_model.excalidraw|400]]

---
# Many to Many (N:M)

- Entity in A associated with N entity in B. While entity in B also associated with N entity in A.
- Example: Customer buys product, Student attend
