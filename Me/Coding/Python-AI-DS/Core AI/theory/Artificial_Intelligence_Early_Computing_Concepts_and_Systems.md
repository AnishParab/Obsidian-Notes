# Era: 1930s–1940s — theoretical foundations of computation

**Computation Theory**
- Alan Turing → Turing Machine: abstract model defining what is computable
- Established limits of computation (halting problem)

**Lambda Calculus**
- Alonzo Church → formal system for computation via function abstraction and application
- Church-Turing thesis: both models capture the same notion of computability

**Cybernetics**
- Norbert Wiener → study of feedback, control, and regulation in systems
- Framed intelligence as a feedback-driven process — influenced later AI and control theory
