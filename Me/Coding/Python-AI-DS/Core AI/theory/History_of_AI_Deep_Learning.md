# History of Deep Learning
### 1960s — Perceptron Era

- Introduced by Frank Rosenblatt
- Single-layer linear model

**Limitation:**
- Cannot represent non-linear functions (e.g., XOR)

---
### 1969 — First AI Winter

- Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert showed:
    - Perceptrons cannot learn XOR

→ Loss of interest and funding

---
### 1980s — First Revival

- Driven by Geoffrey Hinton
**Key idea:**
- Backpropagation (learning internal representations)

---
### 1989 — CNNs

- Yann LeCun
- Applied CNNs to:
    - Handwritten digit recognition (ZIP codes)

---
### 1990s — Second AI Winter

- Limited data
- Poor initialization + training instability
- Rise of simpler models (e.g., Random Forests)

---
### 2006 — Deep Learning Foundations

- Geoffrey Hinton
**Key idea:**
- Unsupervised pre-training
- Enabled training of deeper networks

---
### 2012 — Breakthrough (Modern DL)

- ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge

**Key factors:**
- GPU training
- Large labeled datasets
- Deep CNNs outperform traditional methods

**Notable contributions:**
- Ian Goodfellow → GANs
- DeepMind → large-scale DL systems

---
### Post-2012 — Scaling Era

- Data ↑ (internet-scale datasets)
- Compute ↑ (GPUs/TPUs)
- Frameworks:
    - TensorFlow
    - PyTorch

→ Deep learning becomes dominant

---
# Summary

- Early DL failed due to:
    - **theoretical limits + lack of compute/data**
- Modern DL succeeded due to:
    - **scale (data + compute) + better training methods**
