# Harmonic Series

When you play A4 (440Hz) on a violin, you're not just hearing 440Hz. You're hearing:
``` text
Partial 1 (fundamental) →  440Hz  (1 × 440)
Partial 2               →  880Hz  (2 × 440)
Partial 3               → 1320Hz  (3 × 440)
Partial 4               → 1760Hz  (4 × 440)
...
```

> Each partial is an integer multiple of the fundamental. The **relative amplitudes** of these partials is what makes a violin sound like a violin and not a flute. That amplitude profile is called the **harmonic spectrum** or **timbre**.
