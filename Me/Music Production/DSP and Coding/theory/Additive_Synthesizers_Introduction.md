# Additive Synthesizers

- Sound is air pressure changing over time.
- Any sound — no matter how complex — can be decomposed into a sum of sine waves at different **frequencies**, **amplitudes**, and **phases**.
- This is **Fourier's theorem**.
- Additive synthesis exploits this in reverse:
	- Build complex timbres by adding simple sine waves together.

> Additive synthesizer uses only **sine wave oscillators**, which sum to produce the synthesizer's resultant sound.
