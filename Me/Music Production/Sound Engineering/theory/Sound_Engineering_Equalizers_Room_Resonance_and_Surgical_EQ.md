# Room Resonance

- Every room has natural frequencies that it amplifies due to its shape and size
- These frequencies "ring" or "build up" — called resonant frequencies or room modes
- Result: certain frequencies sound louder than they actually are in your mix
- What sounds balanced in your room may sound bass-heavy or harsh elsewhere

---
**How to Overcome Room Resonance**

- Identify the problematic frequency using a spectrum analyzer or by sweeping a bell filter
- Narrow the Q (high Q = surgical) to isolate the exact resonant frequency
- Cut (don't boost) at that frequency — you're removing what the room is adding
- Use a notch filter for severe resonances — precise, minimal collateral damage
- Gentle broad cuts work better for wider room mode buildup (low Q)
- Rule of thumb: cut narrow, boost wide

---
# Surgical EQ

- Surgical EQ = using a very high Q (narrow band) to precisely target and cut a single problematic frequency
- Used when a frequency is clearly identifiable — resonance, hum, harsh spike
- Technique: boost with high Q first to make the problem frequency obvious → find it → switch to cut
- Always cut, never boost surgically — boosting narrow bands sounds unnatural
- Common use cases: room modes, mic resonance, string/fret noise, electrical hum (50/60 Hz)
